GOALS

This visualization examines 100 years of sci-fi cinema through four lenses: narrative motifs, cultural anxieties, imaginative distance, and world-building ambition. By combining film metadata, historical timelines, and geographical mapping, we explore how our visions of the future evolve across decades.

Our goal is to help users understand:

  • What sci-fi chooses to worry about
  • When imagination expands or contracts
  • How cultural moments shape the futures we depict
  • Why certain tropes keep reappearing

This project was created at Harvard as part of a larger investigation into innovation, storytelling, and collective imagination. It merges data visualization, narrative design, and cinematic analysis into a single interactive experience.
